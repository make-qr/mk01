Monkey Mart Upgrades: Best Order to Unlock

Which Monkey Mart upgrades should you buy first? This guide ranks every early and mid-game upgrade so you grow faster with fewer wasted coins.

Upgrades are the engine of Monkey Mart progression. Spend coins wisely and your store scales smoothly; spend randomly and you will fight empty shelves with a bigger floor you cannot fill. This guide gives you a proven Monkey Mart upgrade order used by experienced players.

Priority 1: Movement Speed

Buy speed level 1 and 2 before anything else. Every task in Monkey Mart involves walking — harvest, stock, collect money, buy upgrades. Faster movement compounds across hundreds of trips per session.

Priority 2: Carry Capacity

Upgrade capacity level 1 and 2 right after speed. Carrying more bananas and corn per trip means fewer back-and-forth runs between fields and shelves. Speed without capacity still wastes time on extra journeys.

Priority 3: Unlock Corn

Your first new product should be corn. It adds a second revenue stream per customer visit. Keep banana shelves full while you learn the corn harvest-and-stock loop before unlocking eggs or milk.

Priority 4: Speed Level 3

With two product lines active, you need even faster movement. Buy speed 3 before expanding the store or hiring helpers — you must personally cover both banana and corn routes efficiently first.

Priority 5: First Helper Monkey

Hire when shelves go empty while you harvest. The helper should cover your worst bottleneck — usually banana harvesting or corn stocking. More detail: when to hire helper monkeys.

Priority 6: Eggs and Milk

Unlock eggs when corn runs smoothly with helper support. Add milk after eggs stay stocked for several customer waves without your direct help.

Priority 7: Store Expansion

Expand only when current shelves stay full during peak traffic. A bigger store without helpers and speed is a bigger problem. See store expansion guide.

Upgrades to Avoid Early

  • Floor expansion before speed 3
  • Third product line before second stays stocked
  • Multiple helpers before one works efficiently
  • Decorative upgrades over functional speed/capacity

Late Game Upgrades

Processed goods — flour, bread, snacks — need full production chains. Upgrade each chain link before adding the next product. Assign dedicated helpers per zone and max out speed before opening new floors.
