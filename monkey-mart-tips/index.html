Best Early Game Upgrades

Most players progress faster with this upgrade priority in the first 15 minutes:

  1. Movement speed (level 1–2) — cuts travel time between fields and shelves; the single best early buy
  2. Carry capacity (level 1–2) — doubles or triples products per trip; pairs perfectly with speed
  3. Corn unlock — adds a second product line with higher per-customer revenue than bananas alone
  4. Speed level 3 — before any floor expansion; you need to move fast to cover two product types
  5. First helper monkey — only after speed and capacity are solid

Product Unlock Order

Unlock products that match customer demand before spending on floor expansion. Recommended order for most players:

  • Bananas — starter product, always keep stocked
  • Corn — first unlock; reliable mid-game income
  • Eggs — after corn shelves stay full without your help
  • Milk — pairs with bakery items later
  • Flour and bread — late-game high-value chain

A well-stocked small store beats a large store with empty shelves. See the complete product list: all products in Monkey Mart.

When to Hire Helper Monkeys

Hire helpers when you consistently have spare coins after restocking and you miss sales because shelves go empty while you harvest. Signs you are ready:

  • You watch corn shelves go empty while picking bananas
  • Register money piles up because you are always in the fields
  • You can afford a helper without dropping below zero coins afterward

Your first helper should cover your biggest bottleneck — usually harvesting or stocking. Detailed timing guide: when to hire helper monkeys.

Coin Farming Tips

  • Never let your best-selling shelves sit empty — bananas first, then corn
  • Collect register money before buying large upgrades
  • Focus on one upgrade goal per short play session
  • Run tight loops: harvest → stock → collect → upgrade → repeat
  • Unlock new products only when current shelves stay full for 2+ customer waves

For a full early-game coin plan, read Monkey Mart early game strategy.

Store Layout Tips

Keep production areas close to their matching shelves. Bananas grow behind the store — stock the nearest banana display first. When you add corn, place its shelf along your shortest walking path from the corn patch to the register.

After expansion, assign each helper to a zone: one monkey on crops, one on shelf restocking, one near checkout during rush periods. Avoid spreading high-demand items across opposite ends of the mart unless a helper covers the route.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Expanding the store too early — more shelves to fill with the same speed
  • Skipping capacity upgrades — slow trips waste speed bonuses
  • Unlocking products you cannot keep in stock — angry empty shelves hurt income
  • Hiring helpers before speed upgrades — you still bottleneck on personal movement
  • Ignoring the register — uncollected coins do not count toward upgrades

Mid Game Strategy (After First Helper)

Once one helper covers harvesting or stocking, shift your focus to the remaining bottleneck. Upgrade speed again before adding a second product line. Unlock eggs or milk only when corn runs smoothly with helper support.

Start planning your second helper when customer traffic outpaces a single automated worker. Mid game is about depth before width — max out current products before expanding floors.

Late Game Strategy

In the late game, assign clear roles to multiple helpers: harvesters, stockers, and register support. Balance expansion with upgrade depth — a third floor means nothing if bread shelves sit empty.

Prioritize high-traffic shelves during busy periods. Processed goods (flour, bread, snacks) earn more per customer but need longer production chains. Build the chain one link at a time rather than unlocking everything at once.
